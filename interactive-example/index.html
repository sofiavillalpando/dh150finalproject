Interactive Example

To demonstrate a struggle that a mixed student may face, I created an interactive story in the point of view of a young Chicana-identifying woman who is applying for an internship. She is an undergraduate student hoping to gain experience in the field she wants to pursue a career in. However, as she is applying, she is faced with the challenge to choose ONE race/ethnicity to identify with. 

This game is inspired by The Mujerista, revolving around an article she wrote about being mixed and the struggles that come with it.

Why is Identity Important?

Identity is more than a word describing your background. According to Merriam-Webster, it is "the distinguishing character or personality of an individual." A significant characteristic that defines a person's identity is their race/ethnicity, and mixed students, being more than one race/ethnicity, are faced with the challenge to choose the identity they will be accepted with most.

Mental Health Effects

As all minorities, mixed students face severe mental health effects regarding their upbringing and backlash from their communities. However, mixed students are often overlooked because many of them have traits that give them privilege; for instance, being white-passing. When I came to UCLA, I was intrigued with the amount of diversity and cultural clubs there were, but there were little resources specifically made for mixed students. Being mixed often come with the challenge of deciding which cultural affinity you resonate with most; and that is decided based on the environment you are in. In an article published by the National Library of Medicine, researchers found that "Adolescents who self-identify as more than 1 race are at higher health and behavior risks. The findings are compatible with interpreting the elevated risk of mixed race as associated with stress." Regardless of what a mixed student identifies as, there is always the chance of backlash from their communities.
